Problem Statement (In Words)

Software Cheesemongers is a company that builds software for small dairy shops in Italy. 
These shops sell different types of cheeses, and the system keeps track of each cheese in the inventory.
Each cheese item has two properties:
validByDays – the number of days remaining before the cheese expires.
quality – the value or freshness of the cheese.
Every day the system updates all cheeses in the inventory by adjusting their validByDays and quality values according to specific rules.

General Rules

1) At the end of each day, the validByDays value of most cheeses decreases by 1.

2) The quality of most cheeses decreases by 1 each day as they get closer to expiration.

3) Once the validByDays becomes less than 0 (the cheese has expired), the quality decreases five times faster than before.

4) The quality of a cheese can never be negative.

5) The quality of a cheese can never exceed 100.

6) Special Cheese Types

Some cheeses behave differently:

#Parmigiano Regiano

This cheese improves with age.
Instead of decreasing, its quality increases over time.

#Caciocavallo Podolico

This is a legendary cheese.
Its quality never decreases, and its validByDays value does not change.

#Tasting with Chef Massimo

This represents a special cheese-tasting event.
As the event date approaches, the quality increases.
When there are 14 days or fewer, the quality increases faster.
When there are 7 days or fewer, it increases even faster.
After the event is over (validByDays < 0), the quality drops to 0.

New Requirement

The system must now support a new cheese called Ricotta.
Ricotta spoils faster than normal cheeses, so:
Its quality decreases three times faster than normal cheeses.
